Sambal Udang

A bold and aromatic Malaysian dish featuring succulent prawns in a spicy chili-petai sambal.

Ingredients

  • 500g fresh prawns, peeled and deveined (keep tails on)
  • 100g petai (stink beans), sliced
  • 8–10 red chilies, soaked in hot water
  • 5 shallots
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 tsp belacan (shrimp paste), toasted
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste, mixed with 2 tbsp water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the sambal paste: Blend red chilies, shallots, garlic, and belacan into a smooth paste.
  2. Heat oil in a wok or pan over medium heat.
  3. Sauté sliced onion until translucent.
  4. Add the chili paste and stir-fry for 5–7 minutes until fragrant and oil starts to separate.
  5. Add prawns and cook for 2–3 minutes until they turn pink.
  6. Stir in petai and cook for another 2 minutes.
  7. Pour in tamarind water, add sugar and salt. Simmer for 3–5 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  8. Serve hot with steamed white rice.

This dish is best enjoyed with family — the bold flavors are unforgettable!
