Nasi Lemak

Malaysia’s beloved national breakfast — fragrant, spicy, and deeply satisfying.

Ingredients

For the Rice:

  • 2 cups rice
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 pandan leaf, knotted
  • 1 tsp salt

For the Sambal:

  • 10 red chilies, soaked
  • 3 shallots
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 tsp belacan (shrimp paste), toasted
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil

For Serving:

  • Fried anchovies
  • Roasted peanuts
  • Boiled egg
  • Sliced cucumber

Instructions

  1. Wash the rice until water runs clear. Drain.
  2. Cook the rice: In a pot, combine rice, coconut milk, pandan leaf, and salt. Cook as you would normal rice until fluffy.
  3. Prepare sambal: Blend chilies, shallots, garlic, and belacan into a smooth paste.
  4. Heat oil in a pan. Fry sliced onion until soft.
  5. Add chili paste and stir-fry for 5–7 minutes until fragrant.
  6. Mix in tamarind paste, sugar, and a bit of water. Simmer for 10 minutes until oil separates.
  7. Serve hot coconut rice with sambal, anchovies, peanuts, egg, and cucumber.

Enjoy this taste of Malaysia with your family!
